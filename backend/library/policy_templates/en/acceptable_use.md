# Acceptable Use Policy

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines the acceptable use of information technology resources within the organization. It aims to protect the organization and its employees from risks associated with improper use of technology.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, consultants, and third parties who access the organization's IT resources, including but not limited to computers, networks, email, internet, and mobile devices.

## 3. General Use

### 3.1 Authorized Use
- IT resources are provided primarily for business purposes
- Limited personal use is permitted provided it does not interfere with work duties or violate this policy
- Users are responsible for exercising good judgment regarding personal use

### 3.2 Prohibited Activities
Users shall not:
- Use IT resources for illegal activities
- Access, download, or distribute offensive or inappropriate material
- Attempt to bypass security controls or access unauthorized systems
- Install unauthorized software without IT approval
- Share credentials or allow others to use their accounts

## 4. Email and Communications

- Email shall be used responsibly and professionally
- Sensitive information shall be encrypted when transmitted externally
- Users shall not open suspicious attachments or click unknown links
- Auto-forwarding of email to external addresses is prohibited
- Mass distribution of non-business emails is not permitted

## 5. Internet Usage

- Internet access is provided for business-related activities
- Streaming services should not be used in ways that degrade network performance
- Downloading of unauthorized or pirated content is strictly prohibited
- Users should exercise caution when browsing and avoid untrusted websites

## 6. Mobile and Remote Working

- Mobile devices used for business must be secured with a passcode or biometric authentication
- Lost or stolen devices must be reported immediately
- Public Wi-Fi networks shall not be used for accessing sensitive data without a VPN
- Remote workers must ensure their work environment maintains confidentiality

## 7. Data Handling

- Data shall be handled in accordance with its classification level
- Sensitive data shall not be stored on personal devices without authorization
- Data shall not be transferred to external media without approval
- Cloud storage services must be approved by IT before use

## 8. Monitoring

The organization reserves the right to monitor the use of its IT resources to ensure compliance with this policy. Monitoring may include but is not limited to network traffic, email content, and internet usage logs.

## 9. Enforcement

Violation of this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment. Criminal activities will be referred to law enforcement authorities.

## 10. Policy Review

This policy shall be reviewed annually and updated as necessary to reflect changes in technology, regulations, or organizational needs.
