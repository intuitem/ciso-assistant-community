# Incident Response Policy

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the framework for identifying, managing, and resolving information security incidents. It ensures a consistent and effective approach to incident handling, minimizing impact and enabling rapid recovery.

## 2. Scope

This policy covers all information security incidents affecting the organization's systems, data, personnel, or operations, including but not limited to:
- Unauthorized access to systems or data
- Malware infections
- Data breaches or data loss
- Denial of service attacks
- Social engineering attacks
- Physical security breaches impacting information assets

## 3. Incident Classification

### 3.1 Severity Levels

| Level | Description | Examples |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| Critical | Significant impact on business operations or data | Data breach, ransomware, complete system outage |
| High | Major impact requiring immediate attention | Unauthorized access to sensitive data, partial outage |
| Medium | Moderate impact with limited scope | Malware on a single system, policy violation |
| Low | Minor impact, no data loss | Failed intrusion attempt, minor policy deviation |

## 4. Incident Response Phases

### 4.1 Preparation
- Maintain an incident response team with defined roles and responsibilities
- Ensure team members are trained and conduct regular exercises
- Maintain up-to-date contact lists and escalation procedures
- Deploy and maintain detection and monitoring tools

### 4.2 Detection and Analysis
- Monitor systems and networks for indicators of compromise
- Analyze alerts to determine scope and severity
- Document initial findings and classify the incident
- Notify appropriate stakeholders based on severity

### 4.3 Containment
- Implement short-term containment to limit immediate damage
- Preserve evidence for forensic analysis
- Implement long-term containment measures as needed
- Communicate containment status to stakeholders

### 4.4 Eradication
- Identify and eliminate the root cause
- Remove malware, unauthorized access, or other threats
- Patch vulnerabilities that were exploited
- Verify that the threat has been completely removed

### 4.5 Recovery
- Restore affected systems from clean backups
- Verify system integrity before returning to production
- Monitor restored systems for signs of recurring issues
- Gradually restore operations with enhanced monitoring

### 4.6 Post-Incident Review
- Conduct a post-incident review within 5 business days
- Document lessons learned and recommendations
- Update policies, procedures, and controls as needed
- Share relevant findings with appropriate teams

## 5. Reporting Requirements

- All suspected incidents shall be reported to the security team immediately
- Critical and high severity incidents shall be reported to senior management within 4 hours
- Regulatory notifications shall be made within required timeframes
- Affected individuals shall be notified as required by law

## 6. Roles and Responsibilities

### 6.1 Incident Response Team
- Lead incident investigation and coordination
- Make containment and eradication decisions
- Produce incident reports and recommendations

### 6.2 All Employees
- Report suspected incidents promptly
- Cooperate with incident investigations
- Follow instructions from the incident response team

### 6.3 Management
- Provide resources and support for incident response
- Make business decisions related to incident impact
- Approve communications to external parties

## 7. Evidence Handling

- Evidence shall be collected and preserved following forensic best practices
- Chain of custody shall be maintained and documented
- Evidence shall be stored securely and retained per legal requirements

## 8. Communication

- Internal communications shall follow established escalation procedures
- External communications (media, regulators, customers) shall be coordinated through authorized spokespersons
- Communication templates shall be maintained and updated regularly

## 9. Policy Review

This policy shall be reviewed at least annually and after every major security incident. The incident response plan shall be tested through tabletop exercises at least twice per year.
